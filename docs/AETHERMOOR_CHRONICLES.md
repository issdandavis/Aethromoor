## OVERVIEW

**Aethermoor** is the Prime World of Mortals, the First Realm in the Spiral of Pollyoneth multiverse. It is an ancient world with a rich, cyclical history marked by Ages of transformation, magical awakenings, and the rise and fall of civilizations.

### Key Characteristics:

- **Age:** Ancient, with over 3,000 years of recorded history
- **Current Year:** 3124 (Modern Era)
- **Magic Level:** High, enhanced by Second Awakening
- **Government:** Circle of Speakers (unified council)
- **Peoples:** Five major races (Sylvani, Drakmori, Nordgrim, Sunward Folk, Deepstone Folk)
- **Structure:** Multiple continents, diverse climates and biomes
- **Special Features:** Great Crystals, Resonance Network, Leyline Highways

### Historical Pattern:

Aethermoor's history follows a cyclical pattern of:
1. **Awakening** - Surge of magical power
2. **Growth** - Civilization expansion
3. **Conflict** - War or crisis
4. **Unity** - Coming together
5. **Renewal** - New age begins

This pattern has repeated three times, with a prophesied Third Awakening approaching.

## THE AGE OF DAWN (Years 1-847)

### The First Awakening (Year 1)

**The Beginning:**
In the beginning, the world of Aethermoor was shrouded in **eternal twilight**. No sun had ever risen, no dawn had broken the perpetual gray dusk that covered the land.

**The Luminal Awakening:**
The **Great Crystals of Lumina**, buried deep within five sacred mountains, suddenly ignited with divine light. This miraculous event:
- Brought the **first dawn** to Aethermoor
- Illuminated the world with natural sunlight
- Stirred consciousness in scattered tribes
- Marked the beginning of **recorded history**

**The Five Sacred Mountains:**
1. Mount Pyraxis (East - Volcanic)
2. Mount Lumora (West - Forest)
3. Mount Glacius (North - Ice)
4. Mount Solara (South - Desert)
5. Mount Terra (Center - Plains)

Each mountain housed one of the Great Crystals, and each became sacred to different peoples.

---

### Rise of the First Peoples

The Luminal Awakening stirred consciousness across Aethermoor, giving birth to the sentient races:

#### **The Sylvani** (Forest Folk)
- **Origin:** Emerged from the Whispering Forests in the west
- **Affinity:** Nature magic
- **Culture:** Tree-dwelling, harmonious with nature
- **Magic:** Plant growth, animal communion, natural healing

#### **The Drakmori** (Dragon-Blood)
- **Origin:** Descended from the volcanic peaks of Mount Pyraxis
- **Affinity:** Fire and stone magic
- **Culture:** Master smiths and warriors
- **Magic:** Metalworking, fire manipulation, earth shaping

#### **The Nordgrim** (Northern People)
- **Origin:** Carved their halls from the Frozen Reaches in the north
- **Affinity:** Ice and metal
- **Culture:** Hardy craftspeople and traders
- **Magic:** Frost magic, metal enhancement, preservation

#### **The Sunward Folk** (Desert Nomads)
- **Origin:** Established first cities in the fertile Southern Plains
- **Affinity:** Sun and wind magic
- **Culture:** Agricultural innovators, traders
- **Magic:** Weather control, agriculture enhancement, divination

### The Covenant of Elements (Year 412)

**The Problem:**
As magical abilities manifested differently among the peoples, territorial conflicts arose. Each race claimed lands based on their elemental affinities, leading to:
- Border disputes
- Resource conflicts
- Cultural misunderstandings
- Magical duels and skirmishes

**The Solution:**
The great mage **Arcanum the Unifier** brokered the **Covenant of Elements**, a revolutionary peace treaty that:

**Established:**
- The first **inter-racial council**
- Division of Aethermoor into **four great kingdoms** based on elemental affinities
- Foundation for **magical law**
- Neutral zones for trade and diplomacy

**Results:**
- Treaty lasted **over 400 years**
- Enabled cultural exchange
- Standardized magical practices
- Created Common Tongue precursor

---

### The Dragon Wars (Years 623-689)

**The Dragon Awakening:**
Ancient dragons, slumbering since before the First Awakening, were roused by the growing magical energies permeating Aethermoor. These primordial beings:
- Had existed since before the dawn
- Were vastly more powerful than mortal mages
- Claimed territories without regard for the Covenant

**Major Dragon Lords:**
- **Vorthak, the Crimson Terror** - Claimed the Eastern Wastes
- **Icewhisper Kyraleth** - Dominated the northern glaciers
- **Earthshaker Dramoth** - Controlled mountain ranges
- **Skydancer Aeris** - Ruled the upper atmosphere

**The Mortal Response:**
The kingdoms united under **High King Valorian the Dragonbane**, who:
- Forged the first multi-racial army
- Led decades of warfare against the dragons
- Lost thousands of soldiers
- Finally negotiated from a position of strength

**The Treaty of Flame and Scale (Year 689):**
- Established permanent **dragon territories**
- Dragons agreed not to attack mortal settlements
- Mortals agreed to respect dragon domains
- Created framework for future cooperation
- Remains respected to this day

**Legacy:**
- Proved that mortal unity could match ancient power
- Established High King as military leader
- Created lasting respect between dragons and mortals

### The Plague of Shadows (Years 1287-1294)

**Origin:** Unknown (suspected void corruption)

**The Blight:**
A mysterious wave of **dark magic** swept across the Southern Plains:
- **Withered crops** across thousands of acres
- **Corrupted the land** itself
- Turned fertile soil black and lifeless
- Spread like a disease

**Shadow Spawn:**
The plague created twisted creatures called **Shadow Spawn**:
- Born from corrupted soil
- Fed on life energy
- Multiplied rapidly
- Resisted conventional magic

**The Crisis:**
- Thousands died of starvation
- Sunward Federation faced collapse
- Military intervention failed
- Magic seemed powerless

**The Solution:**
**Saint Lyralei the Lightbringer**, a priestess of the Light, discovered that:
- Pure life force could purify the corruption
- Required ultimate sacrifice
- Would save the realm but cost her life

**The Sacrifice:**
Lyralei performed a ritual of **supreme purification**:
- Gave her entire life force
- Created a wave of cleansing light
- Purified most affected regions
- Destroyed the Shadow Spawn

**Legacy:**
- Sunward Federation saved
- Lyralei became a saint and martyr
- **Shadowscars** remain in some areas:
  - Patches of permanently corrupted land
  - Cannot support life
  - Serve as warning
  - Studied by mages for insight into dark magic

### The War of Succession (Years 1456-1463)

**The Succession Crisis:**
When **High King Valorian III** died without a clear heir:
- All four kingdoms claimed the right to rule
- Ancient bloodlines were debated
- Political marriages contested
- No clear successor emerged

**The War of the Five Crowns:**
Each kingdom **claimed the right to rule** the others:
- Sylvani argued for wisdom and age
- Drakmori claimed strength and power
- Nordgrim cited wealth and resources
- Sunward Folk emphasized population
- Free Cities sought independence

**Seven Years of Conflict:**
- Battles across multiple fronts
- Economic devastation
- Magical warfare
- Political intrigue and betrayal

**The Resolution:**
The **Concord of Equals** (Year 1463):
- Abolished the High Kingship
- Established the **High Council of Aethermoor**
- **Rotating leadership** instead of monarchy:
  - Each kingdom leads for 5 years
  - Council makes major decisions together
  - Unanimous vote for war
- Equal representation

**Impact:**
- More stable than monarchy
- Prevented future succession wars
- Increased cooperation
- Lasted nearly 1,000 years

### The Elemental Wars (Years 1789-1834)

**The Discovery:**
**Quintessence Crystals** found deep underground:
- **Pure magical energy** in solid form
- Could power spells for years
- Amplified magical abilities
- Nearly unlimited potential

**The Conflict:**
The most **devastating war** in Aethermoor's history:
- Each kingdom sought control
- Magical weapons of mass destruction used
- Landscapes permanently altered
- Millions died

**War Crimes:**
- Entire cities destroyed
- Magical plagues unleashed
- Reality itself warped in battle zones
- Forbidden magics employed

**The Shocking Revelation:**
The Quintessence Crystals were **sentient**:
- Had consciousness and will
- Were observing mortal behavior
- Judged mortals unworthy
- Made a decision

**The Scattering:**
The Crystals **scattered themselves** across Aethermoor:
- Hid in impossible-to-reach locations
- Placed themselves under powerful enchantments
- Left only fragments accessible
- Created trials for those seeking them

**Resolution:**
With the prize gone, the war **ended in exhaustion**:
- No victor
- All sides devastated
- Forced cooperation to rebuild
- Led to greater unity

- **Spread madness:** Mental contamination

**The Response:**
**Order of the Silver Flame** founded:
- Dedicated to combating void threats
- Developed specialized techniques:
  - **Void purification** rituals
  - **Dimensional sealing** magic
  - **Possession detection** spells
  - **Sanctification** of corrupted areas

**The Struggle:**
Twenty-one years of constant vigilance:
- Void incursions became less frequent
- Detection methods improved
- Most rifts sealed
- Eternal watchfulness required

**Legacy:**
- Order of the Silver Flame remains active
- Void threat never fully eliminated
- Dimensional magic regulated
- Greater awareness of cosmic dangers

### The Sundering of Pyraxis (Year 2234)

**The Catastrophe:**
**Mount Pyraxis**, sacred volcano of the Drakmori, erupted catastrophically:
- **Void corruption** had infected volcanic core
- Explosion visible across entire continent
- Blast reshaped the eastern landscape
- Thousands died instantly

**Immediate Effects:**
- **Scattered the Drakmori people** across Aethermoor
- Destroyed Pyratheon, their capital
- Created the **Ashen Barrens** (permanently volcanic wasteland)
- Initiated **Ember Rain** phenomenon (still occurs)

**Ember Rain:**
Magical weather phenomenon in affected region:
- Glowing ash falls like rain
- Harmless but eerie
- Contains residual fire magic
- Harvested for enchanting

**Diaspora:**
Drakmori scattered to:
- Other kingdoms (refugees)
- Free Cities (new communities)
- Underground (with Nordgrim)
- Remote areas (independent settlements)

**Cultural Impact:**
- Loss of homeland created unity among survivors
- Drakmori preserved culture despite dispersion
- Became more integrated with other peoples
- Dragon-blood magic evolved

- Morteus's phylactery location

- Gave his life to cast ultimate anti-necromancy spell
- Created the **Blessed Barrier**
- Destroyed Morteus's phylactery

Permanent magical effect covering Aethermoor:
- Prevents **large-scale necromancy**
- Individual raising of dead still possible
- Mass undead armies impossible

- Dawnbreaker revered as greatest paladin

**Timeline:**
- Izack born in Aethermoor (exact year unknown, likely around 3090)
- Trained at Grand Academy on Celestia
- Specialized in dimensional magic
- Discovered Cave of Dimensional Resonance

- Avalon becomes separate but connected realm

| Aspect | Aethermoor | Avalon |

| **Magic** | Structured, institutional | Collaborative, conscious |
| **Education** | Traditional academy | Living realm teaches |
| **Time** | Cyclical epochs | Living construct |
| **Boundaries** | Sacred but unstable | Meant to be transcended |
| **Society** | Established, hierarchical | Adaptive, experimental |

**Ongoing Connection:**
- Avalon students sometimes from Aethermoor
- Grand Academy aware of Avalon's existence
- Order of Silver Flame monitors Avalon (dimensional safety)
- Knowledge exchange occurs
- Some Aethermoor mages study at Avalon
- Expeditions in game visit Aethermoor locations

Prophecy suggests Third Awakening in Aethermoor might be same event as dimensional convergence Avalon prepares for. Both realms approaching cosmic transformation.
