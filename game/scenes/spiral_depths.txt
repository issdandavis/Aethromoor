Beneath the council chambers, where architecture surrenders to geology and constructed reality gives way to something that predates intention, ancient pathways wind into the Spiral's heart. Few are granted passage here—not because access is jealously guarded, but because finding these passages requires perception that transcends ordinary sight. The stairs you descended exist in that liminal space where physical and conceptual overlap, accessible only to those who have demonstrated they can navigate ambiguity without demanding false certainty.

Time flows differently in these depths. Not slower or faster precisely, but thematically rather than sequentially. Moments expand to accommodate significance, compress when nothing consequential occurs. You could spend what feels like hours here and emerge to find minutes passed above, or vice versa. The Spiral operates according to narrative logic in these foundational chambers, organizing experience by meaning rather than mechanical chronology.

The walls shimmer with crystallized memories—not metaphorical, but literal. Experiences preserved in mineral form, each facet containing compressed understanding that can be absorbed through touch or contemplation. The accumulated wisdom of everyone who ever walked these paths exists here in condensed form, available to those willing to learn from generations past without demanding they repeat every mistake personally.

Phosphorescent fungi provide illumination that doesn't quite behave like light from conventional sources—it seems to emanate from different temporal angles simultaneously, casting shadows that suggest multiple possible futures overlapping in the present moment. The air tastes like deep earth and old water and something metallic-sweet that might be concentrated magic leaching from stone saturated with power accumulated across centuries.
